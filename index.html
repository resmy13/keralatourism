About Kerala

Kerala, often referred to as "God's Own Country," is a mesmerizing state located in the southwestern part of India. Renowned for its lush greenery, serene backwaters, pristine beaches, and vibrant culture, Kerala is a destination that captivates travelers from around the world. This page offers a glimpse into the rich tapestry of Kerala, covering its geography, culture, history, and major attractions.

Places To Visit

Kerala, India's enchanting southwestern state, is a treasure trove of breathtaking destinations. From the tranquil backwaters of Alleppey and Kumarakom to the misty hills of Munnar, Kerala offers an array of places to explore. The historic city of Kochi, with its blend of colonial architecture and bustling spice markets, is a must-visit. The pristine beaches of Kovalam and Varkala invite you to unwind by the Arabian Sea.

Things To Do

Tourists in Kerala are spoiled for choice when it comes to activities and experiences. One of the top attractions is embarking on a tranquil backwater cruise in Alleppey or Kumarakom, where you can savor the serene beauty of the region from the comfort of a traditional houseboat. Exploring the picturesque tea plantations and hiking amidst the misty hills of Munnar is a paradise for nature enthusiasts.

Accomodation

Tourists visiting Kerala have a wide range of accommodation options to suit every preference and budget. For those seeking luxury and indulgence, there are opulent five-star resorts and boutique hotels nestled in lush greenery, along serene backwaters, or right by the pristine beaches. These accommodations offer world-class amenities, rejuvenating spas, and exquisite dining experiences.

About Us

The Kerala Tourism Department is the government body responsible for promoting and
overseeing tourism activities in the Indian state of Kerala. Established to harness and showcase the
immense tourism potential of Kerala, this department plays a pivotal role in shaping Kerala as
one of India's most popular tourist destinations.
